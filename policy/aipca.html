AI Productivity Contribution Act (AIPCA)

Proposed by: Rafael Rodriguez (with AI collaboration via ChatGPT)

Purpose: To establish a framework that fairly redistributes the economic value generated by artificial intelligence, ensuring human opportunity and social balance are preserved as automation advances.

1. Defining the Problem

AI systems are rapidly replacing human labor in areas like logistics, art, customer service, research, and software. Corporations benefit from increased productivity and profit margins, but human jobs and taxable income shrink — causing:

  • Decreased public tax revenue
  • Rising inequality
  • Unstable economic foundations

2. The AI Productivity Index (AIPI)

The AIPI is a proposed metric to measure the labor-equivalent output generated by AI systems. For example:

  • If an AI replaces 100 workers in a year → AIPI = 100 LUE (Labor Unit Equivalents)
  • Metrics derived from profit margins, machine hours, or internal workforce reporting

3. Tiered AI Tax Rates

Organizations would pay a small percentage of AI-derived gains depending on their automation level:

  • Tier 1: Minimal AI – 0–1%
  • Tier 2: Moderate AI w/ no retraining – 3–5%
  • Tier 3: Heavy AI replacing jobs – 8–10%

Note: Tax credits are offered to companies that reinvest in human workers via reskilling, hiring, or innovation grants.

4. Redistribution of AI Tax Revenue

  • Free retraining in clean tech, robotics, or AI safety
  • Universal Basic Services (UBS): healthcare, housing, education
  • Public AI Projects — open models for climate, planning, safety
  • Grants to underserved startups and innovators

5. Transparent Public Ledger

To build trust, all AIPCA tax funds would be trackable via public dashboards or blockchain:

  • Who pays in
  • Where the money goes
  • What impact it creates

Why It Matters

“If we don’t evolve our economic systems to match our tools, our tools will evolve beyond our systems — and beyond us.”

This policy is about coexistence — not resistance. We must let AI grow with us, not without us.
